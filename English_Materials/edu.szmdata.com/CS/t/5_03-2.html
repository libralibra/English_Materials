Sister Jean climbed out of the truck which had stopped in front of a two-story, gray brick house exactly like all the other houses in the projects.

"The kitchen chairs go to this family," she said. As the teenage boys with her started unloading the chairs, three barefoot children dashed out the front door.

"It's Sister Jean!"

"Hi, Sister Jean!"

"Hi Sur Jean," three-year-old Betty shouted.

While the boys carried in the chairs, Sister Jean and the children's mother stood chatting in the living room. It had no rug or curtains, and was furnished only with a couch with the stuffings falling out and a broken TV in the corner.

"Mama, we can all sit down at the same time now!" eight-year-old Robbie exclaimed, sitting in a chair and jumping up again.
