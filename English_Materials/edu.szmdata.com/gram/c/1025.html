6.126 'of'

'Of' can be used in prepositional phrases after any noun to indicate various relationships between one noun group and another, especially belonging, possession, and connection. It can be used to state what something is, what it contains, what it is made from, or how much of it there is.

  • He was a member of the golf club.
  • She's a friend of Stephen's.
  • ...the Mayor of Moscow.
