4.63 emphasis

You can make a negative statement more emphatic by using 'never' followed by 'do', 'does', or 'did' in front of the base form of the verb. For example, instead of saying 'I never met him', you can say 'I never did meet him'.

  • I never do see her now.
  • We never did find the gauze pad.
  • I never did want a council house.
  • Some people never do adjust adequately.
