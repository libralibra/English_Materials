5.108 simultaneous events

To indicate that two or more events happen at the same time, the adverbs 'together' and 'simultaneously', or the adjuncts 'at the same time' and 'at once' can be used:

  • Everything had happened together.
  • His fear and his hate grew simultaneously.
  • Can you love two women at the same time?
  • I can't be everywhere at once.
