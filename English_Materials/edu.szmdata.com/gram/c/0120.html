1.135 qualifiers

When you want to give more information about the person or thing referred to by an indefinite pronoun, you can do so by using a qualifier, for example a prepositional phrase or a relative clause.

  • I changed the plan and made the talks open to everyone over twelve.
  • He would much rather have somebody who had a background in the humanities.

For more information about qualifiers, see paragraphs 2.289 to 2.320.
