10.80 conjunctions

When people are speaking or writing informally, they often add an extra piece of information using one of the conjunctions 'and', 'but', 'yet', 'or', and 'nor' to begin a new sentence.

  • We stayed fifteen miles from Bonar Bridge. And we went up the coast too.
  • I think it's motor cycling. But I'm not sure.
  • It's not improving their character. Nor their home life.
