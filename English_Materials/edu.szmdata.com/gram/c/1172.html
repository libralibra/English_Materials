8.126 past participle: earlier events

If you want to say what happened to someone or something before a situation or event described in the main clause, you say what happened in a clause containing a past participle on its own.

  • The novels of Mary Webb, praised by Stanley Baldwin and so popular in the 30s, were great favourites of mine.
  • Angered by the policies of the union, she wrote a letter to the General Secretary.
