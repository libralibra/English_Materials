2.144 submodifiers used for making comparisons

When you want to suggest that you are comparing different amounts of a quality, you can use submodifiers such as 'comparatively', 'relatively', and 'equally'.

  • Psychology's a comparatively new subject.
  • The costs remained relatively low.
  • Her technique was less dramatic than Ann's, but equally effective.
  • He was extra polite to his superiors.

Submodifiers are explained in paragraphs 2.145 to 2.173.
