2.41 emphasizing disapproval

A small group of adjectives ending in '-ing' are used in very informal spoken English for emphasis, usually to indicate disapproval or contempt.

  • Everybody in the whole stinking town was loaded with money.
  • The flaming car's locked.

Here is a list of adjectives used informally for emphasis:

  • blinking, blithering, blooming, blundering, crashing, flaming, freezing, piddling, raving, scalding, stinking, thumping, thundering, whopping
