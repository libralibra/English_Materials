3.114 transitive three-word phrasal verbs

A few three-word phrasal verbs contain a transitive verb. The direct object of the verb comes immediately after the verb. A second noun group is put after the preposition, as normal.

  • Multinational companies can play individual markets off against each other.
  • Kroop tried to talk her out of it.
  • I'll take you up on that generous invitation.

Here is a list of three-word phrasal verbs which contain a transitive verb:

  • do out of, frighten out of, let in for, let in on, play off against, put down as, put down to, put up to, take out on, take up on, talk out of
