2.61 two or more adjectives

When you use two adjectives as the complement of a link verb, you use a conjunction, usually 'and', to link them. If you use more than two adjectives as a complement, you usually put a conjunction between the last two adjectives and commas between the others. This is fully explained in paragraphs 8.168 to 8.174 and paragraph 8.189.

  • The room was large and square.
  • We felt hot, tired, and thirsty.

Note that you put the adjectives in the order that you think is the most important.
