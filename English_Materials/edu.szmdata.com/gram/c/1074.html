7.67 USAGE NOTE

There are a few expressions containing impersonal 'it' which are used as reporting clauses before 'that'-clauses to indicate that someone suddenly thought of something: 'It occurred to me', 'It struck me', and 'It crossed my mind'.

  • It occurred to him that he hadn't eaten anything since the night before his capture.
  • It crossed my mind that somebody must have been keeping things secret.
