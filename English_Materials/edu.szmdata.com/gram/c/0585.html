3.211 transitive verbs with an infinitive without 'to'

A few verbs are followed by an object and an infinitive without 'to', not a present participle or a 'to'-infinitive. They are 'let', 'make', and 'have' in the sense of 'cause to happen' or 'experience'.

  • Jenny let him talk.
  • My father made me go for the interview.
  • ...so that he could lie in a darkened room and have her bring him meals on trays.

Verbs which can be used either with an infinitive without 'to' or with a present participle are explained in paragraph 3.206.
