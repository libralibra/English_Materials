10.7 adjuncts

There are also several types of adjunct which you can use to focus on a clause as a whole, or on different elements of the clause. These include sentence adjuncts (see the section beginning at paragraph 10.56), linking adjuncts (see paragraphs 10.73 to 10.81), and focusing adverbs (see paragraphs 10.87 to 10.91).

  • He never writes, of course.
  • This can be done reasonably easily. Still, it's not a nice idea.
  • As a child she was particularly close to her elder sister.
