1.75 '-ess' and '-woman'

Words that refer to women often end in '-ess', for example 'actress', 'waitress', and 'hostess'. Another ending is '-woman', as in 'policewoman' and 'needlewoman'.

  • ...his wife Susannah, a former air stewardess.
  • A policewoman dragged me out of the crowd.
  • ...Margaret Downes, who is this year's chairwoman of the examination committee.
