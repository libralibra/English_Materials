Penicillin breaking the mould

Alice and Neil discuss penicillin, the so-called wonder drug discovered in 1928 by Alexander Fleming. They talk about Neil's sore toe, mouldy coffee mugs, and why drug companies should be looking at ways to extend the lifespan of antibiotics and find new ones.

This week's question

How many lives penicillin has saved since its first use as a medicine in 1942? Is it…

a) 20 million?

b) 200 million?

c) 2 billion?

You'll hear the answer at the end of the programme.

Vocabulary

antibiotic
substance that kills microorganisms

halo
circle

mould
Soft, green substance that develops on food or on objects that have been left for too long in warm moist air

chemotherapy
chemical treatment used to kill cancer cells

suppresses
stops

immune system
our body's defence against infection and disease

pave the way
make something possible

resistance
ability to remain unharmed

corpses
dead bodies
